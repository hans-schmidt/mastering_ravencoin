Blog 2021-09-02: Reactions to the Evermore Defi Roadmap Proposal

The Evermore Defi Roadmap proposal published last week stimulated a torrent of public reactions, DMs, and emails. All of the messages stated opinions, and many requested further explanation from me.

Before attempting to satisfy those requests, I would like to express my gratitude for the overall positive attitude toward me in the messages I received. Most people know that I have never requested donations or any compensation for the work which I do on Ravencoin. I knew when I decided to include a funding proposal together with the technical content, that I was "kicking the hornet nest" in order to study the reaction. While researchers who use that strategy may learn important information, it can be difficult to feel much empathy when they get stung. Yet, although many people expressed disapproval of the funding portion of the proposal, every single respondent took effort to make clear that the criticisms were targeted at the proposal and not at me, and to thank me for my efforts. Not even the most vitriolic members of the community attacked me personally, which is a very positive statement about the quality of the community. Thank you for your restraint and for your objectiveness.

Now, at the risk of further antagonizing the hornets and getting stung after all, I will document a few of my opinions and conclusions:

1) Some facts about the fee-based development fund proposal:

I chose the published funding proposal because I judged it to be the least problematic and most practical of the available funding choices. However, in addition to any concerns related to centralization, it is a fairly weak economic plan. Contrary to the concern expressed by some people that it would be a money-grab by developers, it would have a good chance of failing due to providing too little money. The initial unburning of asset fees would provide about $1.5 Million, which is enough to sustain a small development team for one or two years. But after that, the funds provided by fees would be insufficient unless activity on the network increased by about 150x. If the developers could in fact make that happen, then they would surely have more than justified their salaries.

The primary advantage of a fees-based Development Fund is that it would provide a built-in alignment of incentives in the same way that stock options do in companies. Even after increasing transaction fees last year by 100x because they were too close to dust, they add up to less than $300 per week. Ravencoin adoption for real-world use cases other than mining has been an abysmal failure and most blocks are nearly empty.

It is worth mentioning that concerns were raised about whether a fee-based Development Fund would endanger Ravencoin's status as a currency rather than a security. Currently, those are speculations which may or may not be legitimate concerns. It does seem appropriate and prudent for the Ravencoin Foundation to seek expert legal advice on this topic.


2) Some analysis of the community reaction:

Many members of the Ravencoin community view Ravencoin first and foremost as a strictly-decentralized GPU-mined semi-clone of bitcoin. They strongly believe that Ravencoin must never compromise on any aspect inconsistent with the bitcoin mindset. The open-source maximally-decentralize design must persist. Bitcoin's only governance is the miners. Satoshi did not get paid for his code development efforts. Those should hold for Ravencoin, they say.

Yet many of those same members focus their public communication on Ravencoin's asset capabilities and complain of Ravencoin's lack of participation in Defi.

Let me be clear: Bitcoin does not and cannot participate in Defi. Being a Defi participant and being a bitcoin semi-clone are mutually exclusive goals.
  • Defi has developed into a fast-moving innovative high-transaction-volume business which requires very active code development and funding mechanisms to pay for those developers.
  • Bitcoin is primarily a store of value rather than a method of exchange. It moves slowly and carefully. Code developers are nice to have, but are not particularly needed.

Evermore was designed for people who want Ravencoin to become a leading participant in Defi, which requires compromises in decentralization, governance, and development funding. It is my belief that Ravencoin is far better suited than Ethereum to deliver the promises of Defi with decent security. I also believe that Defi represents Ravencoin's best chance of maximum price and market cap. But not everyone wants those changes or agrees with that prediction.

Ravencoin could have value as an alternative store of value based on GPU mining. Ravencoin's coinbase halving next January and the growing crowd of GPU miners being forced off of Ethereum may provide sufficient reasons for Ravencoin's price and market cap to grow dramatically in the coming months. The fact that virtually all Ravencoin miners run proprietary closed-source mining software shows the limits of their commitment to open source. The fact that Ravencoin was founded based on some ideas about blockchain assets may become an irrelevant historic fact which almost nobody uses. There will always be hobbyists, of course, just as there are still 1980-vintage Atari video game enthusiasts. But that is unrelated to Defi. 

Some community members have expressed the opinion that a Developer Fund should be set up using community donations. This is certainly worth exploring, but the amount of money needed would be far more than ever raised before. Moreover, talented developers would not consider such a system competitive and worthy of risking their careers unless there was sufficient confidence that funding would continue on an ongoing basis.

Another option would be for code development to be in a Corporation funded by private Venture Capital. There has been some discussion of building a proprietary closed-source permissioned network on top of Ravencoin using merge mining. It is likely that miners would cooperate for even a small additional amount of income. This may prove to be the most practical path forward, but it is the farthest from the principles on which Ravencoin was founded.

It is a market truism that the economic value of something is equal to how much people are willing to pay for it. That sends an unambiguous message about the value of code development on Ravencoin.

  • These are all very different visions for the future of Ravencoin.
  • If you are a Ravencoin hodler or miner, I cannot tell you the future. Your crystal ball is as good as mine.
  • But if you are a talented code developer who wants to work on a fast moving blockchain project which will value your contribution, then I can provide some guidance about where to work and where not to waste your efforts at this point in time.
